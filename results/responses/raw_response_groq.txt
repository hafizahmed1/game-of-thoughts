Here are the rules of Tic Tac Toe explained in bullet points:

* The game is played on a 3x3 grid, created by drawing two horizontal parallel lines intersected by two vertical parallel lines, resulting in 9 spaces.
* The game is played by two players, Player One and Player Two.
* Player One places Xs in the spaces, while Player Two places Os.
* Players take turns placing their Xs or Os in an unoccupied space on the grid.
* The first player to get three of their symbols in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins the game.
* If all spaces on the grid are filled and no player has won, the game is a draw.
* The grid should be flat on a table, and players can use a pencil, pen, or crayon to create the grid and place their Xs and Os.
* Any method can be used to determine who is Player One, such as Rock Paper Scissors (see separate rules for Rock Paper Scissors).
* Players can only place one X or O per turn, and they must place it in an unoccupied space.
* The game is designed for players aged 4 and up, but there is no upper age limit.

Important constraints:

* The game is only for two players.
* Players must take turns placing their Xs or Os.
* The game is played on a 3x3 grid with 9 spaces.
* Only Xs and Os can be used as symbols.
* The game can only be won by getting three symbols in a row, or it can end in a draw if all spaces are filled and no player has won.
