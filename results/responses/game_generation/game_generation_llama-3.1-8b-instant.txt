**Game Name:** Island Quest

**Objective:** Be the first player to collect three treasures and return to the starting island, while navigating through a treacherous sea route.

**Setup:**

1. The game board consists of a grid representing the sea route, with several islands and a starting island.
2. Each player chooses a game piece (e.g., a small boat) and places it on the starting island.
3. Each player has a set of treasure cards, which are shuffled and placed face down on designated islands.
4. Each player has a set of action cards, which are shuffled and placed face down on designated islands.

**Rules:**

1. On each turn, a player rolls a die to determine how many spaces they can move their game piece.
2. The player can move their game piece to an adjacent island or stay on the current island.
3. If a player lands on an island with a treasure card, they can choose to pick it up and add it to their collection.
4. If a player lands on an island with an action card, they can choose to play it to gain an advantage (e.g., move extra spaces, swap one of their treasures with a treasure on another island).
5. If a player lands on an island with another player's game piece, they can choose to engage in a "battle" by rolling a die and adding a modifier based on the number of treasures they have collected. The player with the higher total wins the battle and can move the other player's game piece to a designated "jail" island.
6. A player can only collect a maximum of three treasures. If they collect a fourth treasure, they must return one of their existing treasures to the starting island.
7. The game ends when one player collects three treasures and returns to the starting island. That player is declared the winner.

**Example Turn:**

Player 1 rolls a 4 on the die and moves their game piece to an island with a treasure card. They choose to pick up the treasure and add it to their collection. They now have two treasures. Player 1 can choose to stay on the current island or move to an adjacent island on their next turn.
