Based on the provided document, here are the rules for the game:

**Setup**
*   **Players:** The game is played by exactly two players (Player One and Player Two).
*   **Equipment:** Use a pencil (or pen/crayon/any writing implement of your choice) and a piece of paper.
*   **The Grid:** Draw two horizontal parallel lines intersected by two vertical parallel lines to create a 3x3 grid (9 spaces). The grid must be placed flat on a table.
*   **Player Assignment:** Determine who is Player One using any method (such as Rock Paper Scissors). The other participant is Player Two.
*   **Markers:** Player One uses Xs and Player Two uses Os. These markers are created virtually in the players' minds and then drawn onto the paper as needed.

**Gameplay**
*   **Turns:** Players take turns placing their respective markers (X for Player One, O for Player Two) into an unoccupied space within the grid.
*   **Constraint:** You cannot place a marker in an occupied space.
*   **Placement:** Markers must be placed inside the 9 spaces created by the grid; the area outside the grid is not valid for placement.

**Winning and Drawing**
*   **Victory:** A player wins if they achieve three of their markers in a row, column, or diagonal.
*   **Draw:** The game is a draw if the grid is completely filled without either player achieving three in a row.

**Additional Constraints**
*   **Age Appropriateness:** The game is intended for players aged 4 and up (though this is a guideline).
*   **Symbols:** Only Xs and Os may be used as markers.
*   **Solo Play:** This is strictly a two-player game; there are no official solo rules.
