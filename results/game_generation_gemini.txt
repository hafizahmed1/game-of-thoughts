### Game Name: **Golden Gulch**

**Theme:** Two treasure hunters explore a shifting, ancient canyon, trying to secure the most gold before the canyon collapses.

---

### 1. Objective
Collect more gold coins than your opponent. The game ends when all gold coins have been collected from the board.

### 2. Setup
*   **The Board:** Use a 5x5 grid (25 squares total).
*   **The Tokens:** Each player chooses a color (Player A: Blue, Player B: Red). Each player has **two** explorer pawns.
*   **The Treasure:** Place 10 gold tokens randomly on the 25 squares. The remaining 15 squares are empty.
*   **Starting Positions:** Players place their two pawns on the two corner squares closest to them (e.g., Player A on bottom-left and bottom-right corners; Player B on top-left and top-right corners).

### 3. Turn Structure
Players alternate turns. On your turn, you must perform two actions in order:
1.  **Move:** Move one of your two pawns exactly one square (orthogonally: up, down, left, or right).
2.  **Shift:** Choose any column or row on the board and "shift" it one space in any direction. The piece that falls off the edge reappears on the opposite side (this is a *wraparound* mechanic).
    *   *Note:* You cannot shift the row or column your own pawn is currently occupying.

### 4. Rules
*   **Collecting Gold:** If you move your pawn onto a square containing a gold token, you collect it and add it to your score pile.
*   **Capturing:** You cannot move onto a square occupied by an opponent’s pawn.
*   **Shifting:** 
    *   If a gold token is in a shifted row/column, it moves with the board space. 
    *   If an opponent’s pawn is in a shifted row/column, it moves with the board space.
*   **Movement Restrictions:** You cannot move diagonally. You cannot move onto a square occupied by another pawn.

### 5. Winning Condition
The game ends immediately when the 10th gold token is collected. The player with the most gold tokens wins. If there is a tie (5-5), the player who collected the final gold token wins.

---

### Strategy Tips
*   **Blocking:** Since you can't shift a row/column your pawn is on, use your second pawn to "guard" a row or column to prevent your opponent from shifting it.
*   **The Shift:** Use the shift mechanic to bring gold closer to your pawns or to push your opponent's pawns away from gold.
*   **Dual Focus:** Because you have two pawns, you can use one to chase gold and the other to obstruct the opponent's path.
