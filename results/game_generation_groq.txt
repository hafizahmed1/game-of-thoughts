**Game Name: Treasure Quest**

**Objective:**
Each player aims to collect and return three treasures to their home base, while hindering their opponent's progress.

**Setup:**
1. The game board features a 10x10 grid, with two home bases (one for each player) located at opposite corners.
2. Each player starts with two pawns (explorers) at their home base.
3. The board contains 12 treasure tokens, randomly placed on the grid, with each token having a point value (1, 2, or 3 points).
4. Each player has a set of five action cards, which can be used to move, block, or swap treasures.

**Turn Structure:**
A player's turn consists of two phases:

1. **Action Phase:** The player draws an action card and can choose to play it or discard it.
2. **Movement Phase:** The player can move one of their explorers up to three spaces in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally).

**Rules:**

1. **Movement:** Explorers can move through empty spaces, but not through spaces occupied by the opponent's explorers or treasures.
2. **Treasure Collection:** When an explorer lands on a space with a treasure token, they can pick it up and carry it with them.
3. **Action Cards:**
	* **Move:** Allows the player to move an additional space.
	* **Block:** Prevents the opponent from moving an explorer on their next turn.
	* **Swap:** Swaps the position of two treasures on the board.
	* **Steal:** Takes a treasure from the opponent's explorer.
	* **Protect:** Prevents the opponent from using an action card on the player's next turn.
4. **Explorer Limit:** Each player can have a maximum of two explorers on the board. If a player has two explorers and wants to move a new one, they must return one of their existing explorers to their home base.
5. **Treasure Limit:** Each explorer can carry a maximum of one treasure token.

**Winning Condition:**
The game ends when one player returns three treasures to their home base. That player is declared the winner. If all treasures have been collected and no player has returned three treasures, the player with the most points (based on the treasure tokens they've collected) wins. In case of a tie, the player with the highest-value treasure token wins.
