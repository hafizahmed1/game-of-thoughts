MODEL RESPONSE
To improve the provided rules for Tic-Tac-Toe, we must address gaps that would leave players uncertain during gameplay.

### 1. Missing or Ambiguous Rules

*   **Turn Order:** The rules do not state who goes first or how the turn order proceeds.
*   **The "Win" Condition:** It mentions "three marks in a row," but fails to define what constitutes a "row" (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal).
*   **Illegal Moves:** It does not specify that a player cannot place a mark in an already occupied square.
*   **Winning vs. Drawing:** It states the game ends when the board is full, but it does not define what happens if the board is full *without* a winner (a draw/cat's game).
*   **Winning Protocol:** It does not state that the game ends *immediately* upon a player getting three in a row.

### 2. Why They Are Problematic

*   **Turn Confusion:** Without defining turns, players might argue over who starts or whether players can take multiple turns in a row.
*   **Invalid Moves:** Without the "occupied square" rule, players could play multiple marks in the same spot, rendering the game board meaningless.
*   **Ambiguous Victory:** If "row" isn't defined, a player might claim a diagonal win is invalid, or conversely, a player might attempt to claim a "win" by placing three marks in an L-shape if the rules aren't explicitly clear.
*   **Draw Ambiguity:** If the game only ends when the board is full, players might play until the board is full even after someone has already won, which contradicts the logic of a competitive game.

### 3. Suggested Corrected Rules

**Tic-Tac-Toe Official Rules**

1.  **The Objective:** The goal is to be the first player to place three of your marks (X or O) in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row on a 3x3 grid.
2.  **Starting the Game:** The game is played by two players. Player 1 is assigned "X" and Player 2 is assigned "O." Player 1 always makes the first move.
3.  **Gameplay:** Players take turns placing their mark in any empty square on the grid. A player may only place one mark per turn.
4.  **Restrictions:** Once a mark is placed in a square, that square is occupied for the remainder of the game. Players cannot place marks in already occupied squares.
5.  **Winning the Game:** The game ends immediately when a player achieves three marks in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally). That player is declared the winner.
6.  **Draws:** If all nine squares are filled and no player has achieved three in a row, the game ends in a draw (often called a "Cat’s Game").
